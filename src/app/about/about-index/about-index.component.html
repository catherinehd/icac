Overview

China Institute of College Admission Counseling (China ICAC) is an organization of professionals from all over the world dedicated to serving Chinese students as they make choices about pursuing postsecondary education overseas.

China ICAC is committed to developing the professionalism of all school based counselors in China by providing a wealth of networking and learning opportunities including but not limited to webinars, workshops, and conferences. China ICAC also effectively bring together high school counselors serving Chinese nationals and college admission officers.

Our Mission

China ICAC is dedicated to promoting SPGP in Chinese schools, helping professional development of school-based college counselors who work with Chinese students, and serving more Chinese students and parents who need college counseling.

Professional Experience

Since 2015, Xiaofeng has been leading the international recruitment efforts at Amherst College where he oversees all aspects of international admissions, including managing the College’s large number of international applications, recruiting on multiple continents, and chairing the international committee. He is also on the College’s domestic admissions team where he recruits in selective US states. Before joining Amherst, Xiaofeng was a member of the admissions office at the College of the Holy Cross, in Worcester, Massachusetts, where he initiated the institution’s admissions strategies in East Asia, recruited domestically in the US, and served on the admissions committee. Xiaofeng is an active member of International ACAC and NEACAC (New England Association for College Admission Counseling) and has presented at the annual conferences of both associations.

Xiaofeng is deeply passionate about international higher education and is committed to connecting US higher education institutions with China, particularly on the admissions side. In 2013, he earned his Master’s degree in Higher Education Administration from Boston College where he served as a graduate admission assistant at the Lynch Graduate School of Education.

Prior to coming to the US in 2011, he was an English broadcast journalist on China Radio International in Beijing, reporting on domestic and international affairs. Xiaofeng is a native from Qingdao, a beautiful coastal city in China’s Shandong Province, and he loves seafood.
